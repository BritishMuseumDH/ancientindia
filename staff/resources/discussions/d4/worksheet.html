Discussion 4

Objectives:
To encourage pupils to think about how we decipher ancient texts and why it is important in increasing our understanding of ancient civilizations. For children to understand how we begin to understand complex ancient scripts.

Materials:
Photographs, books and/or illustrations of a collection of ancient scripts, especially Indus script.

Class set-up:
Whole group discussion

Vocabulary:
syllables, epigrapher, logosyllabic,

Discussion:
Begin the discussion by showing the children pictures of the Indus script and comparing it with another ancient script like Egyptian hieroglyphs. Ask the children if they can see any similarities, but mostly they will be able to see differences.

The children should be able to spot reoccurring figures and symbols.

Do the symbols refer to a picture of something, is it picture writing?

What are the problems of trying to write purely in pictures?

The children could attempt to suggest how we could write a complex message in just pictures, write one with them.

What difficulties would we have? Why would we not understand every message?

WWhat might the symbols refer to if they are not purely pictures? The children may guess that they refer to words and then you could introduce that they may refer to parts of words, sounds or syllables..

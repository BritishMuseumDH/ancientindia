What do the signs mean?

Most of the examples of Indus script that we have today come from stone seals found at Indus Valley sites. Experts over the past hundred years have tried to work out the meaning of the signs on these small seals. However, no one has yet been able to read the signs.

Without a bilingual text or a very long inscription, reading the Indus script is unlikely. However, scholars can compare the writing to other ancient scripts. They can also think about how the seals might actually have been used in order to work out what type of inscription it might be.

Seals were generally used in the ancient world for sealing goods or 'signing' contracts. If the Indus seals were made for this use, the short inscriptions on seals most probably represent titles, proper names, or family names rather than administrative records, personal letters or works of literature.

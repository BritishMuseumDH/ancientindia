Great Bath is constructed at Mohenjo-daro

One of the most famous and recognisable structures from the Indus Valley is the Great Bath at Mohenjo-daro. It was constructed of baked brick and lined with bitumen, which would have made it watertight in antiquity.

The Great Bath measures 12 metres long and 7 metres wide. At its deepest point it is 2.4 metres deep. Archaeologists believe that it was probably used for ritual bathing or religious ceremonies. However, no one knows for sure why it was built or how it was used.

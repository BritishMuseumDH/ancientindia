Spread of Buddhism

In the third century B.C. followers of the Buddha spread his teachings throughout South-east Asia, Central Asia and China. This was largely due to the efforts of the Mauryan emperor Ashoka who sent missionaries to Sri Lanka, Burma, Gandhara, China and South India.

One of the most important missionaries sent out by Ashoka was his son Mahinda. Mahinda travelled to Sri Lanka with a branch of the Bodhi Tree to plant at a newly built monastery

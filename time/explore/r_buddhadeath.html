Death of the Buddha and Vardhamana Mahavira

In 483 B.C. the Buddha died in a town in north-eastern India called Kushinagara. The Buddha had devoted most of his life to travelling great distances and teaching others.

His teachings live on as the religion we call Buddhism today.

Mahavira was the founder of the Jain religion. In 477 B.C. he died at Majjhima Pava, a town near the modern town of Patna. The date of his birth is not known.

Construction of Ellora and Ajanta Caves

In the fifth century, Buddhist cave temples were constructed at the sites of Ellora and Ajanta located in central India.

The Ellora Caves are rock-cut temples dedicated to Buddhist, Jain and Hindu gods. Ellora became a centre of worship for Buddhists, Jains and Hindus.

The Ajanta Caves are famous for the paintings on the walls of the caves. These paintings show scenes from the life of the Buddha as a Bodhisattva.

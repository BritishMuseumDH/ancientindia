Brick-robbing at Harappa

By the mid-nineteenth century, local people had used bricks from the ancient site of Harappa to build the nearby modern village of the same name.

In the 1870s, a railroad was being built from Lahore to Multan. The engineers in charge of laying the track heard about the sturdy bricks from Harappa. Since they needed ballast to anchor the tracks, they investigated the site. For several years, they carted the ancient bricks away in huge numbers. This left the ancient site largely reduced in size.

Early visitors to Harappa

In the early nineteenth century, many western travellers journeyed to India. While there, they visited sites which were well known by local people, but not widely known about in the west.

Several of these early travellers left behind accounts of where they visited and what they saw. At this time, the Indus Valley Civilization was unknown.

In 1826, a man named Charles Masson visited the site of Harappa and wrote a record of what he saw there. He was followed in 1831 by Alexander Burns who was mapping the course of the Indus River.

Between 1850 and 1860, Alexander Cunningham visited Harappa a couple of times. On these visits he explored the area around the citadel mound and found ancient stairs on two sides of the mound.

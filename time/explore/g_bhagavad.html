Composition of the Bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita is a holy book of the Hindus. Hindus believe that people are reborn several times. This book outlines how a person's actions determine what their next life will be like. If a person performs enough good actions it will eventually lead to salvation.

According to tradition, Krishna explained the text of the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna, a Pandava, at the beginning of the battle of the Mahabharata between the Pandavas and the Kauravas.

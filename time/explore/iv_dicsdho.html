Discovery of Dholavira

In the 1980s, archaeologists began excavating at a new Indus Valley site near a village in western India called Dholavira.

Excavations at Dholavira have revealed a city with common Indus Valley features such as a citadel, streets laid out on a grid and the use of complex water drainage systems throughout the city.

One of the most exciting finds from Dholavira is a large inscription. This inscription may originally have been mounted on a board and hung from the gate. This is the largest inscription in the Indus script ever found. Some archaeologists believe that it may represent the name of the city

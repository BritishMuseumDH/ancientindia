Division of Buddhist orders

About 500 years after the Buddha died, two main Buddhist sects disagreed about worshipping the image of the Buddha.

One group called the Hinayana believed that the Buddha's image should not be worshipped. The Buddha's original teachings supported the idea that it was the teachings which were important, not his image.

The second group called the Mahayana wanted to worship the Buddha as a god.

This conflict caused a split in the Buddhist order.

Division of Hindu sects

In the fifth century the Hindu religion was divided into two sects. These sects were called the Vaishnavas and the Saivas.

The Vaishnavas worshipped the god Vishnu in the form of his ten incarnations. They also worshipped Vishnu's consort or wife named Lakshmi who was the goddess of wealth.

The Saivas worshipped the god Shiva. They also worshipped Shiva's consort or wife named Parvati.

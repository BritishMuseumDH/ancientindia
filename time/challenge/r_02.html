Bronze coin

This is a bronze coin from around the first century B.C.

On the front of the coin is an elephant surrounded by a pool with fish in it. This design was often used on early Indian coins. Also on the front of the coin is an inscription in Brahmi script. It reads, "Ra...nisa". Although this inscription is not complete, other coins like this have been found with the inscription "Rano Shri Satakanisa". This means that the coin was probably issued by an early ruler of the Satavahana dynasty called Satakarni.

On the back of the coin is a sacred tree as well as a cross-like symbol representing the capital of the Satavahanas, called Ujjain.

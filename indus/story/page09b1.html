How do we know?

Archaeologists have found several different types of grain at Indus Valley sites. This is an example of some charred grain found at Mohenjo-daro.

Archaeologists have found fish bones in rubbish dumps outside the city walls at some Indus Valley sites. It would make sense that fish was an important part of the diet in this city as it was so close to the river. Dried fish was made for export.

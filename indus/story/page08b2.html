How do we know?

A large inscription was recently found at Dholavira. Some archaeologists believe that it may originally have been mounted on a board outside a gate into the upper town. The 10 signs on the board are 37 centimetres high and 25 - 27 centimetres wide. It is the only example of a large-scale use of the Indus script ever found.

Although we cannot read the Indus script, some archaeologists and scholars believe that the signs may show the name of the town.

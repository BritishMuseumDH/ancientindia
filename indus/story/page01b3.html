How do we know?


Charred grain

Although we do not really know exactly how markets in Indus Valley cities would have worked, archaeologists have found large areas which they believe would have been market-places. Food, grain and luxury goods such as carnelian may have been traded in areas like this.

These open 'market' areas are centrally located and are large enough to hold many people. Sometimes they can only be reached by passing through a large gate. Gates like this would make it possible for officials to control trade in and out of the city and perhaps even to charge taxes on certain types of goods.

Stupa

When the Buddha died, his ashes were divided into eight parts and buried in small caskets inside dome-shaped structures called 'stupas'. For this reason, the stupa is used as a symbol of the Buddha's death.

The remains of important Buddhist dignitaries were also buried in stupas. Thus, images of stupas can also represent important or great people.
